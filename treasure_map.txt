The misty harbors of old Halifax hid a tattered map.

His crew assembled: brave Anna, clever Tom, wise Captain Grey.

Every wave crashed as they sailed on the Swift Gull.

A storm brewed, but they pressed on.

In the fog, an island appeared.

Programmed like a riddle, the map whispered "Beware the guardian."

Raging winds howled, yet they landed safely.

On shore, a cave beckoned with etched puzzles.

Gusts echoed inside as Jack pondered: "I speak without mouth."

Riddles solved, the door opened to jewels.

A spider web blocked the path.

Mighty Tom swung his sword, clearing the way.

At last, they collected coins and gems.

They raced back as the island trembled.

No one could stop their escape to the ship.

Safe aboard, they counted their haul.

Chamber of secrets no more—the quest continued.

Crumbled cliffs behind, new horizons ahead.

Riches beyond dreams cheered Anna as they sailed out.

Out to sea they went, map teasing more adventures.

Crisp winds filled the sails.

Kings would envy, but the true treasure was the journey.
